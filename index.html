  • Royal Galas originated in New Zealand in the 1930s, and are today the world's second most popular variety.

  • Fuji apples not named after Japan's famous volcano, but rather Fujisaki, a town in northern Honshu where they were first bred.

  • Ambrosia apples are a recent cultivar, first identified by the Mennell family of British Columbia in the early 1990s.

  • Granny Smith apples originated in Australia in 1868, and are named after Maria Ann Smith who grew the strain from a chance seedling.

  • The dark-skinned Kingston Black apple originates from Sommerset in the UK, and is mostly used for making cider.

  • Apples come from Central Asia, and today Kazakh wild apples have the greatest genetic diversity in the world. Kazakhstan's largest city, Almaty, is named for the local word for apple.

  • The worlds most cultivated apple is the Red Delicious, though years of agressive selection have taken a toll on the fruit. The thick skin and bitter after taste turn off many consumers.
